Financial Markets · Crypto · Research

I spend most of my time watching markets — not in a passive way, but the kind of watching that turns into questions, and questions that turn into research. Crypto and financial markets have a way of rewarding that kind of obsession, and I've been deep in it long enough to know I'm not going anywhere.

What I do is simple: I follow market trends, dig into the data behind the noise, and write about what I actually find — not what's convenient to say. Corner View is where that work lives. Every week I put out an analysis of what moved, why it moved, and what I think it means going forward. No hedging. No filler.

I'm not here to tell you what to buy. I'm here because I find this genuinely fascinating — the intersection of macro forces, human behaviour, and technology playing out in real time, in a market that never closes. That combination keeps me at the desk longer than anything else ever has.

If something I write makes you think differently about a position, a narrative, or just the week's events — that's enough for me.
