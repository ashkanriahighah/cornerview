My Take on Bitcoin This Week

A personal view on a brutal week — and why I find myself more interested than scared.

I'll be honest with you: this was not a fun week to be watching Bitcoin. And yet, sitting here on 24 June, I find myself more interested in what just happened than I am scared of it. Let me tell you why.

The Week That Rattled Everyone

Bitcoin bled from around $67,000 down to a hair above $59,000. That's a gut-punch move, and the liquidation cascades — over $600 million wiped across crypto — made it feel worse than the numbers alone suggest. On Monday the 23rd, BTC shed over $2,500 in a single session, breaking below a trendline that had been holding since the June 5 rebound. The head-and-shoulders pattern on the 4-hour chart that technicians had been nervously watching all week finally confirmed, pointing toward $57,500 as the next logical target. I won't pretend that's not a real risk.

But here's what I keep coming back to: this didn't happen in a vacuum, and it didn't happen because something broke inside Bitcoin itself.
