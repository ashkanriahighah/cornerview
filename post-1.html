1) This Selloff Came From Outside, Not Inside

When Bitcoin drops, the instinct is to look for an internal failure. There isn't one this time. The Fed has nine of eighteen FOMC members now expecting rate hikes in 2026. AI and semiconductor stocks got crushed. Oil fell on Iran-US deal progress. Over $2.8 billion left Bitcoin ETFs — the worst outflow since the day they launched. Saylor himself said $400 billion rotated into AI infrastructure in six months.

These are external pressures. When BlackRock and Fidelity sell Bitcoin to cover redemptions, the price doesn't care about your long-term thesis. It just falls.

The market isn't broken — it's repricing in a tough macro environment.
